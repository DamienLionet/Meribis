## Technology is never the real cause. Here is what truly makes a Data & AI project succeed.

Too many Data & AI projects fail because they start from a desire to use technology rather than a clearly defined need.
This is not an algorithm problem. It is a problem of strategy, data, and adoption.
Three conditions must be in place for your projects to deliver on their promises all the way to production.

1.  A clear strategy before committing resourcesMany organisations launch AI projects without having answered a simple question: what is the expected value and how will it be measured? Without an answer to that question, Data and AI initiatives remain isolated experiments — difficult to prioritise and impossible to defend in a board meeting.
2.  Data that is genuinely usable, not merely storedHaving data and having AI-ready data are two very different things. Data quality directly determines model quality. This is not a technical constraint to work around: it is the foundation on which the reliability of everything that follows depends.
3.  An industrialisation process that holds over timeThis is the most difficult and most underestimated phase of Data & AI projects. The majority of initiatives stall somewhere between proof of concept and production. Getting past this point demands engineering expertise, business adoption management, and production monitoring that few partners cover end to end.

## Frequently asked questions about Data & AI in the enterprise

**How do I assess the Data & AI maturity of my organisation?**

A structured diagnostic evaluates several dimensions: data quality and accessibility, existing architecture, governance, internal skills, AI practices, and adoption levels. We offer a flash diagnostic in five days or a full audit in two to four weeks depending on the depth required. This diagnostic identifies the prerequisites to address as a priority and the use cases that can be unlocked quickly, without any upfront investment.

**Where do I start when I have no structured AI project yet?**

The most effective starting point is an acculturation and ideation session: understanding what AI can concretely achieve in your context, identifying high-potential use cases, and building a prioritised backlog. This stage takes two to three weeks and prevents investment in the wrong direction. It produces a realistic roadmap, validated with business teams and senior leadership.

**What is the difference between an AI PoC and an industrialised solution?**

A PoC (Proof of Concept) validates the technical feasibility and potential value of an AI use case on a limited scope, with restricted data and a test environment. Industrialisation means making that solution reliable, secure, scalable, and integrated into existing systems, with production monitoring (MLOps). This transition is the most difficult and most underestimated phase of AI projects. It is where the majority of initiatives stall — and where we are frequently brought in.

**How do I secure the use of generative AI within my organisation?**

Generative AI introduces specific risks: hallucinations, leakage of confidential data, decision traceability, regulatory compliance. We help organisations define a clear usage framework, choose appropriate architectures — RAG on private data, open-source models hosted on-premises — and put in place the AI governance necessary for a controlled deployment. Our Innovation unit conducts active research on these topics and has developed OpenRAG, a dedicated open-source framework.

**What are the Data prerequisites for deploying AI in an enterprise?**

Prerequisites vary by use case, but three conditions recur consistently: data that is sufficiently large and representative to train or calibrate models; acceptable data quality (no major duplication, no critical missing values); and infrastructure that provides reliable, secure access to the data. A Data maturity audit enables rapid assessment of these prerequisites and identification of priority workstreams before an AI project is launched.

**How do I measure the ROI of a Data & AI project?**

The ROI of a Data or AI project is measured along two axes: direct gains (productivity improvements, error reduction, task automation, quality gains) and indirect gains (better decision-making, risk reduction, competitive advantage). We work with you to build a value/effort matrix before the project launch, then a ROI calculation model with monitoring indicators tailored to your context. This approach enables you to justify investments to the board and prioritise the most impactful initiatives.

**Which sectors does Meritis work in for Data & AI projects?**

We work across all sectors: banking, insurance & asset management, finance, energy, social housing, industry, cosmetics, retail, online gaming, and the public sector. Through Neofin Advisory, a specialist entity within the group, we also cover financial services with AI solutions adapted to regulatory and compliance constraints.

**What is the difference between a Data & AI consultancy and a technology integrator?**

A consultancy builds the strategy but does not always deliver. An integrator deploys the technology but does not always frame the business challenges. Meritis covers both without any break between advisory and execution: from initial diagnostic through to production, with the same teams from start to finish.

**How do I choose a partner for an end-to-end Data & AI project?**

The determining criteria are: the ability to cover the full chain (strategy, data, development, industrialisation), references on projects that have genuinely reached production, command of governance and compliance, and the ability to manage change through to real adoption by the teams. A partner that can only advise, or only develop, cannot deliver on the end-to-end promise.
