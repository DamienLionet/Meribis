Before deploying a trading strategy, you want to know whether it would have worked in the past. That is the role of backtesting.

## The principle

Backtesting replays a strategy on **historical data** to estimate its performance and risk, without committing real capital.

## The benefits

It allows you to compare strategies, calibrate their parameters and identify periods of potential loss.

## The limits

Overfitting, survivorship bias, neglected transaction costs: a poorly designed backtest gives misleading confidence. Methodological rigour is key.
