Last updated on 3 October 2025.

This policy explains how MERITIS HOLDING and its subsidiaries process personal data collected through this website, its forms, editorial content and audience measurement tools when they are enabled.

## Cookies And Trackers

During browsing, certain cookies or trackers may be used to ensure the website works properly, measure its audience, improve content or enable interaction with third-party services.

Cookies that are not strictly necessary are only placed with your consent. You can accept, refuse or withdraw your consent at any time when the cookie banner or management module is available.

Third-party services, notably social networks accessible from the website, may also place their own cookies if you interact with them and have given consent.

## Data Collected

Personal data that may be collected mainly includes:

- information entered in contact, application or information request forms;
- identification and contact details, such as surname, first name, email address or phone number;
- academic and professional background information as part of an application;
- data related to newsletter subscription or interactions with blog content;
- browsing and audience measurement data, subject to consent when required.

## Purposes Of Processing

Data is used only for the purposes for which it was collected, including:

- responding to a request submitted through the website;
- following up commercially with a prospect interested in Meritis Group services;
- processing an application or unsolicited application;
- measuring website traffic and improving page performance;
- managing subscriptions to communications, events, trade shows or competitions;
- organising candidate, prospect or client outreach when the legal basis allows it.

The legal bases may include consent, performance of a contract or pre-contractual measures, the company's legitimate interest or compliance with a legal obligation.

## Data Recipients

Data is accessible only to authorised people within MERITIS HOLDING and its subsidiaries, depending on their role and the purpose of processing.

It may be transmitted to service providers acting on behalf of the Meritis Group, for example for hosting, forms, audience measurement, event management or recruitment.

As part of recruitment, an anonymised CV in Meritis format may be securely transmitted to a client only after the candidate has agreed.

## Subsidiaries Concerned

Processing may concern MERITIS HOLDING and its subsidiaries, including MERITIS, MERITIS TECHNOLOGIES, MERITIS REGIONS, MERITIS LAB, NAVIGACOM and NEOFIN ADVISORY.

MERITIS HOLDING ensures that its subsidiaries and processors comply with applicable personal data protection requirements.

## Retention Periods

Data is kept for the period necessary for the relevant purpose, then deleted or archived in accordance with legal obligations.

As an indication:

- contact requests are kept for the time required to process them;
- data relating to non-client prospects may be kept for up to three years from the last contact;
- application data may be kept for up to two years, unless the candidate objects or a specific legal obligation applies;
- data required for contract performance is kept throughout the contractual relationship, then archived for the applicable limitation periods.

## Security

Meritis implements technical and organisational measures to protect personal data: secured workstations, protected devices, appropriate passwords, intrusion-prevention tools, backups and confidentiality commitments from employees.

In the event of a personal data breach, MERITIS HOLDING applies the procedures required by the GDPR, including notification to the CNIL and, where required, to the individuals concerned.

## Processors And Tools

Depending on the forms and services enabled, Meritis may rely on external platforms such as form, marketing, survey, recruitment or audience measurement solutions.

The institutional website meritis.fr notably mentions Typeform, HubSpot, Gravity Forms, Google Analytics and Matomo. This static version may use other form services configured in the project.

## Your Rights

In accordance with the GDPR, you have the following rights in particular:

- right to information;
- right of access;
- right to rectification;
- right to erasure;
- right to object;
- right to restriction of processing;
- right to data portability;
- right to define instructions regarding what happens to your data after your death.
