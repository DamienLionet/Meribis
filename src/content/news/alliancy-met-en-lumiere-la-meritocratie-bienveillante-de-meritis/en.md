The media outlet Alliancy dedicates an article to Sébastien Videment, founder and chief executive of Meritis, centred on a key theme: **how to steer an IT services firm through a period of profound digital transformation**, drawing on a management model built around **"benevolent meritocracy"**.

In this interview, Sébastien Videment looks back at the major milestones in Meritis's development:

-   from its initial specialisation in capital markets to sectoral and geographical diversification;
-   through to the recent acquisition of companies such as Neofin Advisory (business consulting and finance) and Navigacom (IT strategy), which strengthen the group's advisory dimension.

![Sébastien VIDEMENT colour photo Meritis](/assets/images/news/alliancy-met-en-lumiere-la-meritocratie-bienveillante-de-meritis/inline-01.webp)

« *Benevolence and negligence are sometimes confused. Yet without demanding standards, performance erodes*. »  
  
Sébastien Videment, Founder of Meritis

A balanced approach is championed to recognise employees whilst maintaining a commitment to performance and accountability. This vision is reflected in Meritis's internal culture, recognised by the Great Place to Work® and B Corp® certifications — hallmarks of a human-centred and sustainable commitment.

In the face of today's major challenges — digital sovereignty, the rise of artificial intelligence, and competitive pressure — Meritis positions itself as an agile and resilient player. Sébastien Videment underlines that AI, however strategically important, will never replace the human value at the heart of his company's model.

His goal is clear: to reach 3,000 to 5,000 employees within ten years, whilst preserving the same high standards and collective spirit that define the group's distinctiveness.
