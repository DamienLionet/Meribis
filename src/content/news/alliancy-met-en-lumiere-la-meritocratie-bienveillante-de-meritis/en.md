<p>The media outlet Alliancy dedicates an article to Sébastien Videment, founder and chief executive of Meritis, centred on a key theme: <strong>how to steer an IT services firm through a period of profound digital transformation</strong>, drawing on a management model built around <strong>"benevolent meritocracy"</strong>.</p>

<p>In this interview, Sébastien Videment looks back at the major milestones in Meritis's development:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>from its initial specialisation in capital markets to sectoral and geographical diversification;</li>

<li>through to the recent acquisition of companies such as Neofin Advisory (business consulting and finance) and Navigacom (IT strategy), which strengthen the group's advisory dimension.</li>
</ul>

<div class="wp-block-media-text is-stacked-on-mobile" style="grid-template-columns:30% auto"><figure class="wp-block-media-text__media"><img width="1024" height="1024" src="/assets/images/news/alliancy-met-en-lumiere-la-meritocratie-bienveillante-de-meritis/inline-01.webp" alt="Sébastien VIDEMENT colour photo Meritis" class="wp-image-56302 size-full" /></figure><div class="wp-block-media-text__content">
<p class="has-text-align-center">« <em>Benevolence and negligence are sometimes confused. Yet without demanding standards, performance erodes</em>. »<br><br>Sébastien Videment, Founder of Meritis</p>
</div></div>

<p>A balanced approach is championed to recognise employees whilst maintaining a commitment to performance and accountability. This vision is reflected in Meritis's internal culture, recognised by the Great Place to Work® and B Corp® certifications — hallmarks of a human-centred and sustainable commitment.</p>

<p>In the face of today's major challenges — digital sovereignty, the rise of artificial intelligence, and competitive pressure — Meritis positions itself as an agile and resilient player. Sébastien Videment underlines that AI, however strategically important, will never replace the human value at the heart of his company's model.</p>

<p>His goal is clear: to reach 3,000 to 5,000 employees within ten years, whilst preserving the same high standards and collective spirit that define the group's distinctiveness.</p>
