<p>The <a href="https://www.masterdevfrance.com/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>Master Dev France 2025</strong> </a>delivered on every promise! This flagship event of the tech community brought together more than <strong>800 developers in competition</strong> and <strong>3,000 enthusiastic visitors</strong>, who came to witness a day defined by performance, innovation and the spirit of sharing.</p>

<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-large is-resized"><img width="1024" height="536" src="/assets/images/news/retour-sur-le-master-dev-france-2025/inline-01.webp" alt="" class="wp-image-54525" style="width:750px" /></figure>
</div>

<p>Having organised our own online competition <a href="#jouer_codeontime"><strong>Code On Time on our new platform</strong></a>, it was our turn to rise to the challenge for the fifth consecutive year at this flagship event! Here is a look back at our participation.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-un-challenge-technique-de-haut-niveau"><strong>A high-level technical challenge</strong></h2>

<p>The <strong>Master Dev France</strong> is a live programming contest that puts the best coding talents to the test through challenges in logic, algorithmics and optimisation. For this 9th edition, participants had the choice of <strong>16 programming languages</strong>, including <strong>Python, JavaScript, Java, PHP, C#</strong> and many others.</p>

<div class="wp-block-columns is-layout-flex wp-container-core-columns-is-layout-9d6595d7 wp-block-columns-is-layout-flex">
<div class="wp-block-column is-layout-flow wp-block-column-is-layout-flow" style="flex-basis:25%">
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img width="200" height="320" src="/assets/images/news/retour-sur-le-master-dev-france-2025/inline-02.webp" alt="" class="wp-image-54526" /></figure>
</div>

<div class="wp-block-column is-layout-flow wp-block-column-is-layout-flow" style="flex-basis:66.66%">
<p>The competition took place in several phases:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Hybrid qualifications</strong>: six 45-minute sessions alternating between algorithmics challenges and optimisation exercises to test speed, precision and code efficiency.</li>

<li><strong>Grand Final</strong>: a concluding optimisation challenge pitting the top participants against one another to crown the winners.</li>
</ul>

<p>This year, a <strong>major new feature</strong> was introduced: the ability for contestants to use <strong>artificial intelligence</strong> as a support tool to solve problems, or to compete solo — placing the confrontation between human ingenuity and emerging technologies at the forefront.</p>
</div>
</div>
