<p><strong>Meritis launches OpenRAG</strong>, an open-source tool for comparing the performance of more than 20 RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) architectures. The goal: to help technical teams make informed decisions for their generative AI projects, based on <strong>concrete, measurable, and reproducible data</strong>.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-benchmarker-les-rag-sur-vos-donnees-en-toute-transparence">Benchmarking RAG systems on your data, with full transparency</h2>

<p>RAG systems have become key building blocks of modern generative AI. They enable LLMs to query an external document base in order to produce more accurate, up-to-date, and contextually relevant responses.</p>

<p>Yet given the diversity of available implementations (LangChain, Haystack, LlamaIndex…), choosing the right pipeline remains a challenge. Until now, no solution made it possible to <strong>objectively compare multiple RAG systems</strong> against concrete criteria such as <strong>response relevance</strong>, <strong>token consumption</strong>, <strong>processing time</strong>, or <strong>energy impact</strong>.</p>

<p>👉 That is precisely what <strong>OpenRAG</strong> offers — the first <strong>open-source RAG benchmark tool</strong>, developed by Meritis's Innovation unit.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What OpenRAG enables</h2>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Compare more than 20 RAG pipelines</strong> under real-world conditions</li>

<li><strong>Test your own business data</strong> in two complementary modes:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><em>Chat Mode</em>: interact with a selected RAG</li>

<li><em>Benchmark Mode</em>: test multiple systems in a structured way</li>
</ul>
</li>

<li><strong>Measure performance</strong> across key criteria:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>response quality (LLM-as-a-judge scoring)</li>

<li>tokens consumed</li>

<li>response time</li>

<li>estimated carbon impact</li>
</ul>
</li>

<li><strong>Visualise results</strong> via an interactive dashboard</li>

<li><strong>Generate actionable reports</strong> to inform your technology decisions</li>
</ul>

<p>The tool ships with a Docker/Conda configuration, compatible with open-source models (Mistral, Ollama, VLLM…) or proprietary ones (OpenAI, Anthropic). It can be deployed locally, on a server, or within a secure environment.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">A platform designed for technical teams</h2>

<p>OpenRAG is aimed at professionals directly involved in the design, evaluation, or industrialisation of RAG pipelines:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>CDOs, Heads of Data</strong>: make technology decisions based on reliable indicators</li>

<li><strong>CTOs, AI architects</strong>: validate a RAG architecture compatible with IS requirements</li>

<li><strong>Data scientists, AI engineers</strong>: test, modify, and customise approaches</li>

<li><strong>AI Product Managers, Innovation Labs</strong>: scope a POC or enrich an MVP with the right components</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">OpenRAG: open source, lean, and impact-oriented</h2>

<p>Released under an MIT licence, <strong>OpenRAG is freely available on GitHub</strong>. It is Meritis's first open-source contribution — Meritis being an IT and Data consulting firm certified B Corp™.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p>« Our ambition with OpenRAG is straightforward: to provide an objective, transparent, and reusable foundation for choosing a RAG system suited to one's business context. It is a public-utility building block for all generative AI teams that want to move beyond the hype. »<br>— <em>Théodore Boullier, Director of Innovation, Meritis</em></p>
</blockquote>

<p>The initiative is part of a broader commitment: to encourage <strong>responsible adoption of generative technologies</strong> by providing lean, auditable tools that can be integrated into demanding environments.</p>
