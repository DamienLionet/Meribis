**Meritis launches OpenRAG**, an open-source tool for comparing the performance of more than 20 RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) architectures. The goal: to help technical teams make informed decisions for their generative AI projects, based on **concrete, measurable, and reproducible data**.

![OPENRAG MERITIS The open-source benchmark for RAG systems](/assets/images/news/openrag-meritis-publie-un-comparateur-open-source-pour-benchmarker-les-systemes-rag/inline-01.webp)

## Benchmarking RAG systems on your data, with full transparency

RAG systems have become key building blocks of modern generative AI. They enable LLMs to query an external document base in order to produce more accurate, up-to-date, and contextually relevant responses.

Yet given the diversity of available implementations (LangChain, Haystack, LlamaIndex…), choosing the right pipeline remains a challenge. Until now, no solution made it possible to **objectively compare multiple RAG systems** against concrete criteria such as **response relevance**, **token consumption**, **processing time**, or **energy impact**.

👉 That is precisely what **OpenRAG** offers — the first **open-source RAG benchmark tool**, developed by Meritis's Innovation unit.

## What OpenRAG enables

-   **Compare more than 20 RAG pipelines** under real-world conditions
-   **Test your own business data** in two complementary modes:
    -   *Chat Mode*: interact with a selected RAG
    -   *Benchmark Mode*: test multiple systems in a structured way
-   **Measure performance** across key criteria:
    -   response quality (LLM-as-a-judge scoring)
    -   tokens consumed
    -   response time
    -   estimated carbon impact
-   **Visualise results** via an interactive dashboard
-   **Generate actionable reports** to inform your technology decisions

The tool ships with a Docker/Conda configuration, compatible with open-source models (Mistral, Ollama, VLLM…) or proprietary ones (OpenAI, Anthropic). It can be deployed locally, on a server, or within a secure environment.

## A platform designed for technical teams

OpenRAG is aimed at professionals directly involved in the design, evaluation, or industrialisation of RAG pipelines:

-   **CDOs, Heads of Data**: make technology decisions based on reliable indicators
-   **CTOs, AI architects**: validate a RAG architecture compatible with IS requirements
-   **Data scientists, AI engineers**: test, modify, and customise approaches
-   **AI Product Managers, Innovation Labs**: scope a POC or enrich an MVP with the right components

## OpenRAG: open source, lean, and impact-oriented

Released under an MIT licence, **OpenRAG is freely available on GitHub**. It is Meritis's first open-source contribution — Meritis being an IT and Data consulting firm certified B Corp™.

> « Our ambition with OpenRAG is straightforward: to provide an objective, transparent, and reusable foundation for choosing a RAG system suited to one's business context. It is a public-utility building block for all generative AI teams that want to move beyond the hype. »  
> — *Théodore Boullier, Director of Innovation, Meritis*

The initiative is part of a broader commitment: to encourage **responsible adoption of generative technologies** by providing lean, auditable tools that can be integrated into demanding environments.
